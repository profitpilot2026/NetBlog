Is $20/Month Actually Worth It?

Everyone wants a clean yes or no on this. Here's the most honest version I can give: if you use ChatGPT for real work more than once a day, Plus pays for itself. If you open it twice a week to ask vague questions, save your $20 and put it toward something else.

But there's more to it than that. The free tier has gotten genuinely better. And what you get with Plus has changed enough that the old reasons to upgrade don't all hold anymore. Let me walk through what actually matters.

What the free plan gets you in 2026

The free plan now includes GPT-4o — which would have been a paid feature a year ago. For most tasks, GPT-4o is excellent. It writes well, reasons clearly, handles complex questions, and feels like a capable AI assistant rather than a watered-down demo.

The catch is consistency. During peak hours — which is basically any weekday from 9am to 7pm — free users get throttled. You'll be mid-conversation and suddenly the responses slow down or switch to an older model. It's not constant, but it's unpredictable enough to be genuinely annoying if you're doing focused work.

Who should pay for Plus

Pay if you tick any of these:

  • You use ChatGPT for actual work — writing, research, coding, planning
  • You need image generation as part of your workflow
  • Peak-hour throttling has disrupted your work even once
  • You're spending more than an hour a week in ChatGPT
  • You want to build Custom GPTs for your specific workflow

Who should stay on free

  • You use it occasionally and rarely hit slowdowns
  • You don't need image generation
  • You're already paying for Claude Pro and the overlap isn't worth it
  • You're still in the "experimenting with AI" phase

"If ChatGPT saves you one hour of work per month, $20 is a rounding error. If it saves you five hours — which it easily can — you're paying four dollars per hour of saved time."

If writing is your main use case, Claude free + ChatGPT free might outperform ChatGPT Plus alone — and cost nothing.
