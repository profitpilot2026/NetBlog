Best AI Writing Tools 2025:
7 We'd Recommend, 3 We'd Skip

We tested every major AI writing tool on the same real content tasks. This is what actually performed — not what had the best landing page.

By Profit Pilot Updated May 2025 10 min read
// 2025 Roundup · Tested hands-on
AI Writing Tools
10 tools tested · 7 recommended · 3 skipped · Updated May 2025

There are now hundreds of AI writing tools. Most of them are wrappers around the same underlying models, dressed up with a template library and a $49/month price tag. Some of them are genuinely useful. Here's what we found after testing ten of the most-recommended options on real content tasks.

Testing criteria: every tool was run on the same blog post brief, email sequence, and ad copy task. We scored on output quality, first-pass usability, instruction-following, and value relative to price.

The 7 we'd recommend

// 01 · Best overall
Claude (Anthropic)
The best writer of the bunch for long-form and nuanced content.
Strengths
  • Best long-form quality
  • Excellent instruction-following
  • 200k context window
  • Great brand voice matching
Weaknesses
  • No image generation
  • Fewer integrations
  • Less punchy for short-form
8.9
/ 10
From Free
Pro: $20/mo
Recommended
// 02 · Most versatile
ChatGPT (OpenAI)
The Swiss Army knife. Best when you need more than just writing.
Strengths
  • Image generation (DALL-E)
  • Web browsing
  • Huge plugin ecosystem
  • Strong short-form copy
Weaknesses
  • Long-form quality slightly below Claude
  • Can be verbose
8.5
/ 10
From Free
Plus: $20/mo
Recommended
// 03 · Best for teams
Jasper AI
Premium price, but the team workflow features justify it at scale.
Strengths
  • Brand voice training
  • Template library
  • Team collaboration tools
  • Structured workflows
Weaknesses
  • $49/mo for solo users is hard to justify
  • Generic phrasing in long-form
7.4
/ 10

Surfer SEO + AI
The only tool that integrates content creation with live SERP data.
Strengths
  • Real-time keyword integration
  • SERP-based content structure
  • NLP optimisation scoring
Weaknesses
  • Writing quality secondary to SEO
  • Pricey for what it is

The 3 we'd skip

// Skip · Overhyped
Writesonic
Promises a lot, delivers mediocre long-form and generic copy.
Acceptable for
  • Very basic short-form drafts
  • Users with minimal budget
Why we'd skip
  • Output quality below ChatGPT at same price
  • Frequent filler and generic phrases
  • Better alternatives exist at every price point
5.8
/ 10

// Bottom line

For most marketers and creators, the answer is Claude Pro or ChatGPT Plus at $20/month — or both at $40/month combined. Everything else on this list is either a team tool (Jasper), a specialist tool (Surfer), or an inferior product at a similar price.
