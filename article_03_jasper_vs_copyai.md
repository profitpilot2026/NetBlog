## Head-to-Head: Five Real Tasks

### Task 1 — 1,500-Word Blog Post From a Brief

**The brief:** Write a post titled "5 Ways Small Businesses Can Use AI to Save Time This Year." Target keyword: AI tools for small business. Tone: practical, no jargon.

**Jasper output:** Structured well, hit the keyword naturally three times in the first 400 words, maintained a consistent tone throughout. The section headers were usable without editing. The examples it used were generic but replaceable. Estimated editing time to publish-ready: 25 minutes.

**Copy.ai output:** Good opening paragraph, then noticeably weaker structure in the middle. The tone shifted between sections — some parts read as casual, others as formal. The keyword appeared once in the first 600 words. Estimated editing time: 45 minutes.

**Winner: Jasper**

---

### Task 2 — Facebook Ad Copy (Three Variants)

**The brief:** A productivity app for freelancers. Price: $9/month. Hook on time-saving. Three variants testing different angles: fear of missing out, social proof, direct benefit.

**Jasper output:** All three variants were distinct in angle and held the word count. The FOMO variant was the strongest. The social proof variant felt slightly templated.

**Copy.ai output:** All three variants were also distinct, but the hooks were sharper. The direct benefit variant — which Copy.ai historically excels at — was cleaner and more punchy than Jasper's equivalent.

**Winner: Copy.ai** — short-form hooks remain its strongest use case.

---

### Task 3 — Five-Email Welcome Sequence

**The brief:** SaaS product for freelance invoicing. Free trial to paid conversion sequence. Emails 1, 3, and 5 are value-focused. Emails 2 and 4 are soft conversion nudges.

**Jasper output:** Clear structure, reasonable subject lines, logical flow from email to email. The conversion nudge emails were a bit timid — they hinted at upgrading rather than making the case. Editing time: 30 minutes.

**Copy.ai output:** The value emails were solid. The conversion emails were actually more direct — Copy.ai's GTM orientation means it is more comfortable with the ask. Subject lines were comparable. Editing time: 35 minutes.

**Winner: Draw** — Jasper on consistency and flow, Copy.ai on conversion copy confidence.

---

### Task 4 — Product Description (E-commerce, 150 words)

**The brief:** A minimalist leather wallet. Premium positioning. Emphasise craftsmanship and durability.

Both tools produced usable output. Copy.ai was slightly more evocative. Jasper was slightly more structured for SEO. Neither required more than 10 minutes of editing.

**Winner: Tie**

---

### Task 5 — LinkedIn Post From a Raw Idea

**The brief:** A rough idea: "AI is not replacing writers, it is raising the bar for average writers."

**Jasper output:** Safe, serviceable, corporate in tone. Would not perform poorly. Would not stop anyone's scroll either.

**Copy.ai output:** More conversational, better hook in the first line, ended with a question that would generate replies. Felt more human.

**Winner: Copy.ai**

## Where Each One Falls Short

**Jasper's weaknesses** are in short punchy copy and anything that needs a human-sounding voice without editing. It is also more expensive, and you will feel that cost if you are not producing content at volume. If you are writing two blog posts a month, you will not get your money's worth.

**Copy.ai's weaknesses** are in sustained long-form quality and brand consistency. Across a 1,500-word article it loses the thread. If you are a content agency producing 20 SEO articles a month, the extra editing time per piece adds up significantly.
