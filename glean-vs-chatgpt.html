Glean vs ChatGPT:
Two Different Problems, Two Different Tools

Most comparisons treat these as competitors. They're not. Understanding why is the key to buying the right one — and not wasting money on the wrong one.

6 min read Updated May 2026 Enterprise focus

The question I keep seeing in enterprise AI discussions is "should we use Glean or ChatGPT?" It's the wrong question. It's a bit like asking whether you should use Google Maps or a calculator. They do different things. The right question is what problem you're actually trying to solve.

Let me lay out what each one actually does — and more importantly, what each one can't do.

What Glean actually is

Glean is a workplace search and knowledge tool. Its entire job is to help people inside a company find information that already exists inside that company — across Slack, Google Drive, Confluence, Jira, email, Notion, and 100+ other tools your org probably uses.

The problem it solves is real and expensive. In most companies over 50 people, a significant amount of working time is spent looking for things. Asking someone where a document is. Searching through Slack history. Re-writing something that was already written six months ago because nobody could find the original. Glean indexes all of it and makes it searchable in plain English.

What Glean doesn't do: create content, answer general questions, write code, or tell you anything about the world outside your organisation's data. It only knows what your company knows.

"Glean is for finding what your company already knows. ChatGPT is for generating things your company doesn't know yet."

What ChatGPT actually is

ChatGPT is a general-purpose AI assistant. It knows an enormous amount about the world — from how to write Python to the history of Renaissance art to how to structure a sales email. It can generate content, answer questions, analyse data, write and debug code, and have genuinely useful conversations about almost any topic.

What ChatGPT doesn't do: access your internal company data. It knows nothing about your Slack messages, your internal docs, your customer data, your processes. Ask it "what did we decide in the Q3 planning meeting?" and it has absolutely no idea.

Who actually needs Glean

The honest answer is: companies above a certain size where internal knowledge is genuinely scattered and hard to find. If you have fewer than 30 people and everyone basically knows where everything is, Glean is overkill.

If you're at a 200-person company where new hires spend their first month asking where things are, where the same questions get answered in Slack over and over, where people re-do work because they couldn't find the original — Glean pays for itself quickly. The ROI calculation is how many hours per employee per week are currently lost searching for information. At enterprise scale, it's usually more than people think.

// The trap to avoid

Companies sometimes buy Glean hoping it'll replace ChatGPT — or buy ChatGPT assuming it covers internal search. Neither works. They solve genuinely different problems and the best-run teams use both.

For everyone else

If you're a solo creator, freelancer, or small team, this comparison probably doesn't apply to you yet. ChatGPT (or Claude) is what you need. Glean is an enterprise product with enterprise pricing and enterprise setup requirements.

Save this article for when your team gets big enough that "where is that doc?" becomes a recurring problem. That's the signal.

// The Verdict

Glean — enterprise teams drowning in internal knowledge they can't find.

ChatGPT — everyone, for general AI work, content, code, research.

Best teams use both. They solve different problems and don't overlap.
