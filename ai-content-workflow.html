Most AI content workflows fail for one of two reasons: they either produce content that sounds like AI wrote it (because no human judgment was applied), or they become so complicated that they take longer than just writing the thing yourself.

What follows is the system we actually use. It's built around a clear principle: AI handles the parts that don't require your expertise, and you handle the parts that do. The result is content that's faster to produce and still sounds like it came from a person who knows what they're talking about.

The 5-stage workflow

01
Idea validation and angle finding
Before writing anything, use Perplexity or ChatGPT (with web browsing) to understand what already exists on the topic. You're not looking for content to copy — you're looking for gaps. What angle is missing? What question isn't being answered well?
✦ Tool: Perplexity AI
// Starter prompt
Search for the top 5 articles about [topic]. Summarise the main angles covered and identify what's missing — what questions are left unanswered or what perspectives aren't represented?
02
Research and fact-gathering
Once you have your angle, use Claude or ChatGPT to pull together the factual scaffolding — statistics, examples, case studies, and key points. The critical thing here is to verify anything specific before it goes in the final piece. AI hallucinates. Your job is to catch it.
✦ Tool: Claude Pro
// Research prompt
I'm writing an article about [topic + your specific angle]. Give me: 5 key facts or statistics (with context for where to verify them), 3 concrete examples or case studies, and the most common misconceptions about this topic.
03
Outline and structure
Don't skip the outline step. An AI-generated outline gives you a structure to react to — you'll move sections, cut some, and add your own. This is faster than building from a blank page and produces better final structure than letting the AI write linearly.
✦ Tool: Claude Pro
// Outline prompt
Create a detailed outline for a [word count] article titled "[your title]" aimed at [audience]. The article should take the angle that [your angle]. Include H2s, H3s where needed, and a brief note on what each section covers.
04
Section-by-section drafting
This is where most people go wrong. They ask the AI to write the whole article at once, then complain the output is generic. Instead, write section by section — paste the outline context, write one section at a time, and inject your own opinions and examples as you go. The sections you write yourself are the ones that make the piece worth reading.
✦ Tool: Claude Pro
05
Edit for voice, then polish
The final step is two passes: first, read through and rewrite any sentence that sounds like an AI wrote it (you'll know the ones). Second, run Grammarly or Hemingway for copy-editing. Don't skip the first pass — no grammar checker will fix generic writing.
✦ Tool: Grammarly / Hemingway

The part AI can't do

The mistake people make with AI content workflows is thinking the AI is the writer. It isn't. You are. The AI is a very fast, very well-read research assistant and first-draft machine. The thing that makes content worth reading — the opinion, the experience, the specific example from your own work — that has to come from you.

If you read through a finished draft and there's nothing in it that only you could have written, the draft isn't done. That's the heuristic. It sounds simple. It's harder to apply than it sounds.

"AI handles the scaffolding. You handle everything that makes the building worth entering."

// Key takeaway

The best AI content workflows are not fully automated. They're AI-accelerated with human judgment at every key decision point. The output is faster and better than either the human or the AI could produce alone.
